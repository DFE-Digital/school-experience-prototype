School experience: types of placement

Each school experience placement is different but there are 3 main types of school experience placement which schools offer.

Depending on the school and the length of your placement, your placement should involve one or all of the following:

  • shadowing a teacher - where you’ll observe a teacher and classroom in action
  • supporting a teacher - where you’ll take on a teaching assistant role and actively take part in classroom activities
  • supervised teaching - where you’ll take on a teaching role and actively lead classroom activities under the supervision of an experienced teacher

However, during your placement you should be presented with the opportunity to at least:

  • observe teaching and pastoral work in action across a range of subjects and age groups
  • talk to teachers about day-to-day school life and life as a teacher
  • observe other areas of teaching outside of the classroom such as departmental meetings and lesson planning

Outline your placement requirements

School experience is not mandatory if you want to become a teacher and there are no set plans as to what each school should offer during a placement.

It’s impossible to set a specific programme suitable for everyone due to each participant’s different level of experience and requirements.

This means, if you do have any specific requirements and things you want to do during your placement, you should raise and arrange these directly with the school.

For example, teaching older pupils or taking part in teaching a certain subject or ability group.

Types of schools

School experience placements are available from all the following types of schools as long as they’re not in special measure:

  • mainstream secondary schools
  • maintained secondary special schools
  • primary schools
  • secondary academies
  • secondary free schools
  • secondary pupil referral units
  • sixth-form colleges

School placement information

Ahead of your school experience placement you should receive the following information from the school:

  • Name and contact details for the person leading your placement
  • School location and access details - including the school’s full address and where you need to access the school site
  • Start and finish times - including a timetable of what you’ll be doing
  • Details of what you need to bring along - For example, proof of identification, any necessary paperwork, pen, paper, laptop etc
  • Whether there’s a charge for your placement - To help cover costs, some schools charge for placements. You’ll need to pay the school direct
